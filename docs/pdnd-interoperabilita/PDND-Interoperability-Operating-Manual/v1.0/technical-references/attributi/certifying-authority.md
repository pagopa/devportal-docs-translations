# Certifying body

### Accreditation and access to functions

Parties can **register** on PDND as **certifying bodies** by submitting a **specific request**.

### Functions and responsibilities of the certifying body

A certifying body can:

* **Create** its own **certified attributes**;
* **Assign** and **revoke** these attributes to or from other parties;
* Allow their **use as access requirements** for e-services, just like other attribute types.

The **responsibility** for the **assignment**, **revocation**, and **accuracy** of certified attributes lies entirely with the **certifying body**.

### Operational benefits and application scope

Certified attributes **simplify** the management of **producing** and enable the certifying body to **build and manage networks of parties** that share specific characteristics useful for defined use cases.

**Example:** **A.N.AC.** acts as a certifying body for **e-procurement**, having visibility not only over its own e-services but also over the **parties** to which it has **assigned attributes** required to access the services connected to **e-procurement workflows**.
