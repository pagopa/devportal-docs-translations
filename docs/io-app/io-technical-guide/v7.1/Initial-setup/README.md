<summary><strong>What is an aggregator entity?</strong></summary>

Entities can join IO as aggregators of other service providers.

_Example: a Region joins IO by aggregating the membership relationship for all local municipalities in the reference territory, making the regional in-house company available to manage the technological integration for all local municipalities._

The aggregation relationship between service providers is governed by agreements (including the cooperation agreements referred to in art. 15 of Law). 241/1990) and by the administrative acts necessary to grant the aggregating entity the powers and attributions required to sign the membership agreement also on behalf of and for the benefit of the aggregated entities, ensuring compliance with the obligations contained therein by the aggregated entities themselves.

The aggregator entity thus ensures compliance with the obligations contained in the membership agreement by the aggregated entities.

Aggregator entities must list all the entities they represent for the purpose of joining IO, using Annex 3 of the [membership agreement](https://io.italia.it/assets/download/it/accordo-di-adesione-IO-2.4.zip), where they must provide the complete list of entities by indicating: name of the represented entity, headquarters, tax code/VAT number, and IPA code.

<mark style="color:red;">**Important!**</mark>

If an entity wishes to independently subscribe to other services (in addition to those made available through the aggregator entity), it must follow the full onboarding procedure by signing the membership agreement [via the Area Riservata.](/broken/pages/yhL4OKIPji8GzDYgcai1).

The aggregator entity can **only** operate for the services for which it has been appointed by the aggregated entity, but it **cannot** act in place of the entity for services for which it has not received a mandate.
