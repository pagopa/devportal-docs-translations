# How to grant a delegation to another party

{% hint style="info" %}
In the _Testing_ and _Production_ environments, delegations are only available to Public Administrations. In the _Verification_ environment, they are instead available to all parties to carry out their own tests.
{% endhint %}

### Step 1: Start the creation flow

Go to _**My party > Delegation management**_. By default, you will land on the _**Delegations granted**_ tab. From there, click _**Create new**_.

### Step 2: Select the type of delegation

Select whether you intend to grant a delegation for producing or for subscribing to an e-service.

### Step 3: Identify the party and the e-service

Identify the e-service for which you intend to grant the delegation. If the delegation being granted is for producing and the e-service for which you want to grant the delegation does not yet exist, you will be asked to provide at least a name and a short description for the e-service before you can grant it in delegation.

Next, identify the party to be delegated. It will be possible to send delegation requests only to parties who have made themselves available by offering themselves as potential delegates. They will still have the right to review and accept the requests.

Finally, you can review and submit the delegation.

{% hint style="info" %}
It is always reminded that the delegating party must appoint the delegate as Data Controller under the GDPR in all cases in which the delegate has access to data belonging to the delegating party or to third parties on behalf of the delegating party.

A dialog box in the front office will remind the parties of this requirement when the delegation is signed.
{% endhint %}
