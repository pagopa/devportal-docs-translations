# Guide to choosing Sign with IO

#### **GLOSSARY**

| Signature type                                              | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Simple Electronic Signature (SES)**    | A signing method involving electronic data, usually in the form of a login and password, logically associated with a document                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Advanced Electronic Signature (AES)**  | A signature that offers greater guarantees than the SES and involves electronic data uniquely linked to the document. The digital signature must be uniquely associated with the signatory in such a way that any alterations to the signed data are immediately detectable. Furthermore, the signature must be generated through the use of a device or method that is under the signatory's sole control |
| **Qualified Electronic Signature (QES)** | The digital signature is the most reliable form of online signature, as it has the same legal value as a handwritten signature and can replace it in any context. To be valid, it must be generated with a specific device (e.g.  such as a USB stick), and must be based on a qualified certificate issued by a qualified trust service provider                       |

#### **1.** WHAT IS SIGN WITH IO\*\*

[**Sign with IO**](https://firma.io.italia.it/) is a service that allows citizens to sign documents through the [IO App](https://io.italia.it/) simply, quickly, and securely, and allows public bodies to manage the entire signing process through a single channel.

Sign with IO is a “one-shot” **Qualified Electronic Signature** with the highest legal value, equivalent to a handwritten signature. This signature is generated by a qualified electronic signature creation device and is based on a qualified certificate for electronic signatures issued by a QTSP (Qualified Trust Service Provider). This QTSP is registered in the [list of qualified trust service providers](https://www.agid.gov.it/it/piattaforme/firma-elettronica-qualificata/prestatori-di-servizi-fiduciari-attivi-in-italia), maintained by AgID according to Art. 29 of the Digital Administration Code.

#### **2.** WHAT IS AN ELECTRONIC SIGNATURE AND WHAT GOVERNS IT\*\*

The use of an electronic signature is an increasingly common practice in the digital context that allows documents to be validated without the need for paper signatures.

💡An electronic signature is not limited to affixing a signature; rather, it is a computerised process that allows data used to identify the signatory to be associated with the electronic document. \
The different types of electronic signatures make it possible to achieve this objective with varying degrees of certainty, while providing legal effects of varying significance.

There are several forms of electronic signature, including the **Simple Electronic Signature (SES)**, the **Advanced Electronic Signature (AES)**, and the **Qualified Electronic Signature (QES)**. Each of these has specific characteristics that make them suitable for different situations and security levels.

To summarise, all three types of electronic signature have different levels of security, integrity, and immutability. The QES has the same characteristics as the Advanced Signature, but it is generated by a qualified electronic signature device and uses a qualified electronic certificate.\
In terms of security, the QES is the **most reliable** signature solution in the digital sphere, comparable to a handwritten signature, as it produces a **reversal of the burden of proof**: in the event of a legal dispute, the signature is considered integral and authentic, and it is therefore up to the party contesting its validity to demonstrate that the signature was obtained fraudulently or that the document has been altered.

There are also [some tools](https://www.agid.gov.it/it/piattaforme/firma-elettronica-qualificata/software-verifica) available online that allow you to verify **the electronic signature**.

#### **3.** WHY CHOOSE SIGN WITH IO\*\*

In view of the above, there are multiple security advantages for public bodies and citizens in choosing Sign with IO.

Advantages for public bodies:

- Sign with IO provides the **greatest security and authenticity**, reducing the risk of fraud and ensuring the integrity of documents;
- Accepting qualified electronic signatures can facilitate compliance with regulations and laws that require specific levels of security in document management;
- **Digitalisation of multi-channel processes** promoting **environmental sustainability**: the transition to electronic signatures reduces paper use and promotes a more sustainable business practice, as well as accelerating workflows, allowing for faster document processing and reducing the time needed to obtain authorised signatures;
- Ability to **track who signed and when**.

Advantages for citizens:

- Ability to **sign remotely** easily and saving time;
- Sign quickly through an already known and reliable tool, the IO App;
- Sign with a signature type that has the **highest level of security** capable of protecting the identity and integrity of the document;
- Use a signature that has **legal equivalence with a paper signature**;
