### What do citizens do?

A citizen who has been asked to sign:

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:blue;">Step 1</mark>: Receive a message on IO or press a button on the public body's website</summary>

The citizen can start the signing process in two ways: by receiving a **message on IO** with the signature request or by pressing the **"Sign with IO" button** on the public body's website.

If they choose the second method and use it from a desktop, they will have to scan the generated QR code with their smartphone's camera.

</details>

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:blue;">Step 2</mark>: Read the document and sign it</summary>

{% hint style="info" %}
**European eIDAS 2 Regulation**

Starting from **May 21, 2026**, in order to ensure greater protection of the signing citizen's identity and in coherence with the new _**European eIDAS2 Regulation**_ _(artt. 24 and 51)_, the user will be required to have a higher authentication level (L3).

Therefore, to proceed with applying a qualified electronic signature (QES) through the IO app, you must authenticate with your physical CIE by holding it near your phone (NFC) and entering the PIN.

This change is a technical adjustment required by law.
{% endhint %}

The citizen views the document on the IO app. They choose which optional clauses to sign and proceed with the **qualified electronic signature (QES)** using the app's unlock code or their biometric recognition.

</details>

<details>

<summary><mark style="color:blue;">Step 3</mark>: Receive the signed document</summary>

They receive the **signed document** through a new message on the IO app.

</details>
